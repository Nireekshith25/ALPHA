Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) Schemes

Karnataka.gov.in

1. Supplementary Nutrition Program (SNP)

The Supplementary Nutrition Program provides additional food to children under the age of 6, pregnant women, and lactating mothers to improve their nutritional status. The program aims to bridge the gap between the Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) and the Average Daily Intake (ADI) of children and women in the target groups. Learn more

2. Immunization

The Immunization program aims to protect children from vaccine-preventable diseases such as polio, diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus, tuberculosis, measles, and hepatitis B. Immunization services are provided at the Anganwadi centers and through outreach sessions. Learn more

3. Health Check-ups

Regular health check-ups are conducted for children under 6, pregnant women, and lactating mothers to monitor their health status. These check-ups include growth monitoring, immunization, treatment of minor illnesses, and referral services for severe health conditions. Learn more

4. Referral Services

The Referral Services scheme provides a link between the Anganwadi center and health facilities to ensure that children and women receive appropriate medical care when needed. Cases that require specialized treatment are referred to hospitals and other healthcare providers. Learn more

5. Pre-school Education (PSE)

Pre-school Education is an essential component of the ICDS scheme. It aims to provide a stimulating environment for the physical, social, emotional, and cognitive development of children aged 3-6 years. Activities include storytelling, rhymes, games, and introduction to basic literacy and numeracy skills. Learn more

6. Nutrition and Health Education (NHE)

The Nutrition and Health Education component aims to educate mothers and caregivers about the importance of nutrition, health, hygiene, and child care practices. It empowers women with the knowledge to make informed decisions about their health and the health of their children. Learn more
